Dictionary

A field guide for translating Gen Z and Gen Alpha expressions into language that will not make Gen X and Millennials stare quietly at a Slack message.

01

I'm/We're cooking

Things are going well; the plan is working and momentum has arrived.

Translation: We're on a roll. Do not disturb the flow state.

02

Peak

Cool, great, top-notch, or the highest available setting of approval.

Translation: Mint, class, excellent, or genuinely rather good.

03

Touch grass

Step away from the internet and reacquaint yourself with offline reality.

Translation: Log off, go outside, and have a small word with yourself.

04

Huzz

A jokey term for girls or potential romantic attention, usually deployed with more confidence than judgement.

Translation: The people someone is trying, perhaps too visibly, to impress.

05

Big back

A teasing or insulting way to call someone greedy, usually because they are eating a lot or acting especially food-focused.

Translation: Someone is being accused of having eyes bigger than their stomach.

06

Tuff

A stylized spelling of tough, usually meaning cool, impressive, resilient, or worthy of respect.

Translation: That is strong, cool, or genuinely impressive.

07

Buzz kill

Someone or something that ruins the mood, interrupts the fun, or drains the energy from a situation.

Translation: The person who has just made the room noticeably less cheerful.

08

Tea

Gossip, drama, or juicy news, especially the interesting details people want to hear.

Translation: The latest story, preferably with the parts everyone is pretending not to want.

09

Rizz up

To charm, flirt with, or successfully attract someone using confidence and social skill.

Translation: To turn on the charm and try to win someone over.
