Selected papers worth keeping close at hand.

Geometric constraints on the architecture of mammalian cortical connectomes

A cross-species connectome model: cortical geometry and resonant eigenmodes reproduce key wiring features in humans, chimpanzees, macaques, marmosets, and mice, pointing to a conserved physical constraint on mammalian cortical architecture.

2026 Cell doi.org

Discovering equations from data: symbolic regression in dynamical systems

A practical symbolic-regression benchmark: five open-source methods are tested across nine dynamical systems, with PySR standing out for recovering governing-equation structure and matching several original trajectories.

2025 arXiv arxiv.org

delta-mem: Efficient Online Memory for Large Language Models

A compact-memory architecture paper: delta-mem attaches an online associative-memory state to a frozen Transformer, using small learned state updates to steer attention without replaying all prior context as text.

2026 arxiv.org

Functional recovery of the adult murine hippocampus after cryopreservation by vitrification

A striking preservation result: adult mouse hippocampal tissue is vitrified, rewarmed, and shown to recover short-term structure, metabolism, excitability, synaptic transmission, and long-term potentiation after molecular mobility had effectively stopped.

2026 Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences doi.org

Hierarchical X-ray microscopy and mesoscopic diffusion MRI in the same brain reveal the human connectome across scales

A standout cross-scale connectomics paper: the same human hemisphere is followed from MRI-scale tractography through hierarchical X-ray microscopy, micro-CT, and electron microscopy, creating a bridge between whole-brain maps and microscopic axonal anatomy.

2026 bioRxiv www.biorxiv.org
