## Summary
This review looks at what would be needed to simulate an entire mammalian brain at cellular detail. The author frames whole-brain simulation as a way to study how large numbers of nonlinear neurons interact to produce brain function. The paper notes that, despite rapid gains in computing, a human whole-brain simulation has not yet been achieved as of 2024. To estimate when such simulations might become feasible, the article examines trends in several related areas. These include supercomputer performance, cell type classification, connectomics, and large-scale neural activity measurements. The review treats these fields as the main bottlenecks because whole-brain simulation depends on both enough computational power and enough biological data. Rather than presenting a new simulation experiment, the paper synthesizes existing technological trajectories. The author then uses these trends to forecast possible timelines for mammalian whole-brain simulation. The central estimate is that mouse whole-brain simulation at the cellular level may be possible around 2034. The paper projects a later timeline for marmoset, around 2044. For human whole-brain simulation, the paper suggests the date is likely later than 2044. A key methodological feature is that these projections are based on trend analysis across multiple enabling technologies rather than on direct experimental validation. The article therefore functions as a forward-looking assessment of feasibility rather than a demonstration that such simulations are imminent.
## Why This Matters
This paper gives a realistic view of what still stands between current neuroscience tools and whole-brain simulation. It is useful for researchers planning long-term work in computation, connectomics, and large-scale brain measurement because it identifies the main technical dependencies.
## Caveats and Limitations
The projections depend on extrapolating current trends, which can break if progress speeds up, slows down, or changes direction. The estimates also appear to rely on broad technological readiness rather than a formal model with uncertainty bounds, so the timelines should be treated as approximate rather than predictive. Because the paper is a review and forecasting exercise, it does not directly test whether the proposed simulation milestones are achievable.
## Key Takeaways
- The paper reviews technological trends relevant to whole-brain simulation.
- It focuses on compute, cell typing, connectomics, and activity measurement.
- The main output is a feasibility timeline, not a new experiment.
- Mouse cellular-level whole-brain simulation is estimated around 2034.
- Marmoset is projected around 2044, and human later than 2044.
