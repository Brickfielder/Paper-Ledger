## Summary
This study presents an extensive post-mortem diffusion MRI dataset from macaque brains and human hemispheres acquired on ultra-high-gradient systems, including Connectome 2.0. By sampling around 50 diffusion shells across diffusion time, echo time, and b-values up to 64,000 s/mm², the authors show that the data can resolve both white matter connectivity and finer cortical and subcortical boundaries at very high spatial detail.
## Why This Matters
The work provides a high-quality imaging resource that can support new methods for diffusion MRI analysis and brain microstructure mapping. It also expands what whole-brain noninvasive imaging can capture in primate tissue, which is useful for both neuroscience research and clinical translation.
## Key Takeaways
- Post-mortem macaque and human samples were scanned for about 250 hours each at very high resolution.
- The protocol combines many diffusion shells with varying b-values, diffusion times, and echo times.
- The resulting data reveal axonal architecture and cytoarchitectonic boundaries beyond standard whole-brain imaging.
