## Summary
This theoretical preprint argues that categorization is not a late labeling step after perception, but an ongoing organizing principle that shapes perception from the beginning. The authors propose that low-dimensional predictive feedback signals help structure high-dimensional incoming sensory inputs, making categorization a situated and anticipatory process rather than static template matching. They integrate ideas from predictive processing, cortical gradients, mixed selectivity, oscillatory dynamics, interoception, and visceromotor control into a single framework. In this account, categorization functions as dimensionality reduction that supports action by constructing multimodal summaries tuned to context and goals. A key claim is that the deepest function of this process is allostasis: efficiently preparing the organism for expected metabolic and behavioral demands. The paper also reframes categories as distributions of context-sensitive prototypes assembled for current and near-future action, not fixed abstract symbols retrieved from storage.

## Why This Matters
The paper offers an integrative framework that links perception, prediction, action, and bodily regulation under one account of categorization. If this view is right, it shifts how we interpret cognition and may generate new hypotheses for neuroscience and computational psychiatry.

## Caveats and Limitations
This is a theory/review paper rather than a new empirical dataset, so most claims are interpretive syntheses of existing evidence. Several central moves, including prioritizing allostatic/limbic control at the top of the predictive hierarchy and mapping computational styles onto psychiatric phenotypes, are promising but remain underdetermined by current direct evidence.

## Key Takeaways
- Categorization is framed as an ongoing predictive process that helps constitute perception, not merely interpret it afterward.
- Top-down feedback is proposed to compress and organize incoming sensory detail into low-dimensional, action-relevant summaries.
- The framework centers allostasis, arguing that prediction and categorization are optimized for energetic efficiency and action readiness.
- Categories are treated as context-sensitive, future-oriented distributions of prototypes rather than fixed representations.
- Extensions to psychopathology are hypothesis-generating and currently stronger as conceptual proposals than validated disorder models.
